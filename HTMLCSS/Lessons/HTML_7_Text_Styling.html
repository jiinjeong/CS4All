Part 1: Bold, Italics, and Highlight

We can make this paragraph text bold, or italisized, or highlighted.

We can make a heading italisized or highlighted, but it is already bold.

We can even make a text that is bold, italisized, AND highlighted.

Part 2: Preformatted Text

Preformatted text appears on your webpage
        almost exactly as it does in your
        HTML document.

This means that it:

  • appears in the computer-esque font 
    Monospace.
  • preserves      indents,
            line breaks,
            and extra   spaces.
  • will go off the screen if you let it go on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on on and on and on and on and on and on and on and on...
    • A scroll bar will appear at the bottom of the page so that you can see any text that extends off of the screen.

To make a paragraph text preformatted:

use only "pre" tags, no "p" tags.

However, you can nest a pre tag inside of a...

heading or

blockquote.

When you open a pre tag, the text will 
always go onto a new line on the webpage.
